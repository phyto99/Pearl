Cellular Automata Philosophy

What makes a good ecosystem simulation? A survey of design philosophies — from what your cells do now, to what they could become when ships start encircling fish.

What You Have Now: Local-Neighbor Lotka-Volterra

Each cell is an autonomous agent. It knows nothing about the world except its immediate 4 neighbors and its own internal counter (RB). From these simple rules, population waves emerge.

Lotka-Volterra — local rules

The Three Laws of Each Tick

① Predator: increment hunger. If hunger > threshold → die into Air. Else try: eat neighbor prey (reset hunger), or clone into Air (1-in-40), or move into Air.

② Prey: try to clone into Air (1-in-N), or move into Air. Only dies when eaten — never of old age.

Key insight: Eating and reproduction are decoupled. Eating only resets hunger. Reproduction is a separate low-probability event. This prevents population explosions.

✓ Simple to implement ✓ Emergent waves ✓ Tunable thresholds ✗ No spatial memory ✗ No schooling

Five Rival Philosophies

Each solves the same problem — emergent ecosystem stability — from a fundamentally different philosophical root.

Philosophy 2
Energy Budget (Metabolic)

Instead of a hunger timer, each cell holds a real energy value. Eating transfers energy. Reproduction costs energy. Death occurs at energy = 0. Movement costs a little energy per step.

Inspired by: Dynamic Energy Theory (Kooijman). Cells are thermodynamic machines.

✓ Physically realistic ✓ Natural stability ✗ Needs more cell bytes ✗ Harder to tune
Philosophy 3
Density-Counted Rules (Game of Life)

Like Conway's Game of Life: a cell lives, dies, or spawns based on counting how many neighbors of each type exist. No internal state — just a lookup table of (myType, neighborCounts) → nextType.

Inspired by: Conway, Brian's Brain, Wireworld. Pure discrete maths.

✓ Zero internal state ✓ Ultra-fast compute ✗ Brittle thresholds ✗ No memory
Philosophy 4
Reaction-Diffusion (Turing)

Two species interact: an activator (prey) auto-catalyses itself AND produces an inhibitor (predator signal). The inhibitor diffuses faster than the activator. Result: self-organising stripes and spots.

Inspired by: Turing 1952. The math behind leopard spots and fish stripes.

✓ Beautiful patterns ✓ Inherently stable ✗ Continuous math in discrete grid ✗ Poor for complex food webs
Philosophy 5
Signalling / Stigmergy

Animals leave chemical trails (pheromones) in cells they pass through. Others follow gradients toward food signals or away from predator signals. Behaviour is indirect — the environment mediates interaction.

Inspired by: Ant colony optimisation, slime moulds, Langton's Ant.

✓ Natural schooling ✓ Prey can sense danger ✗ Trail cells needed ✗ Decay logic required
Philosophy 6
Spatial Lottery / Patch Dynamics

Space is the scarce resource. Each empty cell is a "lottery ticket." Any adjacent reproducing organism can claim it with probability proportional to its reproductive rate. Predation removes organisms, freeing patches.

Inspired by: Hubbells Neutral Theory, Tilman's competition models. Ecological niche theory.

✓ Stable at any scale ✓ Coexistence is natural ✗ Less "behaviour" per cell ✗ Requires global lottery

The Loop-Capture Problem

Ships draw trails. When a trail closes a loop, all fish inside the loop are captured (deleted). This is topologically identical to the Jordan Curve Theorem: a closed curve divides the plane into inside and outside.

Ship closes loop → fish captured

How the Jordan Curve Test Works in a Grid

Ray Casting: From any fish cell, cast a ray in any fixed direction (e.g. →right). Count how many Trail cells it crosses. If the count is odd, the fish is inside the loop.

Flood Fill: From every edge cell, flood-fill Air outward. Any Air not reached = inside. Capture all creatures inside.

Signal Diffusion: Trail cells emit a "barrier signal" into adjacent Air. Fish that can't reach a non-barrier edge within N steps are captured. Most natural for CA.

Ray Cast: fast, O(n) per fish Flood Fill: exact, works for any loop shape Signal: most CA-native, fish can react
Signal diffusion from trail boundary

Signal Diffusion — Most CA-Natural

Trail cells emit a numeric signal into the "outside" Air each tick. The signal propagates outward like a ripple. Any Air cell that never receives the signal is inside the closed loop.

Fish inside get no signal → after N ticks → captured.

Fish outside do get signal → safe. They can also flee away from low-signal zones — pure CA instinct.

What This Means for Your Fish Behaviour

Fish can check: "is my RA (signal strength) above zero?" If yes → move normally. If no → I'm trapped. This requires zero global computation — each cell is self-aware of its enclosure state.

Recommended Philosophy: Hybrid Signalling + Lotka-Volterra

Keep your current Lotka-Volterra hunger model for predator-prey dynamics. Layer on top a boundary signal for the loop mechanic. The two systems operate independently and compose cleanly.

Implementation Architecture

  1. Trail cells are boundary emitters. Each Trail cell writes a signal value into a separate trails[] array (you already have this). Signal value = 255 = "outside marker."
  2. Air diffuses the signal inward. Each tick, Air cells check neighbors. If any neighbor has a signal > 0, copy signal = neighbor_signal - 1 into self. This creates a gradient from Trail outward. Air reachable from edges gets high signal; Air trapped inside a closed loop gets zero signal.
  3. Fish read their cell's signal via RA. When a ship loop closes, inside fish have RA = 0 in their Air neighborhood. After N ticks without signal → change_into HERE Air (captured). Or: a separate "capture sweep" from the ship system.
  4. Prey can flee. Add a check: if right-neighbor signal > my signal, move right (move toward stronger signal = toward open water). This is a pure CA rule — no global state, no flood-fill.
  5. Predators are unaffected. Orca/Sea Lion etc. ignore the signal entirely. They still operate on hunger + prey proximity. The signal layer is purely for the ship-loop mechanic.
The Core Philosophical Statement
🌊
Local Causation
Every decision a cell makes is based only on its immediate neighbors. No cell has global knowledge. The ecosystem emerges from pure locality.
⏱️
Time as Resource
The hunger counter is time-as-scarcity. Predators don't just need prey — they need prey soon enough. This drives hunting urgency without global scheduling.
📡
Signal as Geometry
The trail signal converts a geometric problem (inside/outside a loop) into a local CA question (is my signal zero?). Topology becomes biology.
